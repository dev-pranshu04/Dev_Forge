## Abstract

Proposes a hybrid framework combining automated UI analysis with heuristic evaluation methods to systematically assess the usability of e-commerce mobile applications. The framework reduces reliance on manual expert evaluation while maintaining the depth of heuristic-based assessment.

## Motivation

Manual usability evaluation is expensive, slow, and inconsistent across evaluators. Fully automated tools miss context-dependent usability issues that require semantic understanding. A hybrid approach balances coverage with efficiency.

## Current Status

Under review at Electronic Commerce Research (Springer), 2025. Submitted with full experimental results across 5 e-commerce applications evaluated by 3 expert reviewers using the proposed framework.

## Future Work

Extend framework to non-e-commerce domains. Explore LLM-assisted semantic usability evaluation as a component of the hybrid pipeline.
